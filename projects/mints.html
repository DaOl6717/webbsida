MINTS

A modular integrated network of traffic sensors for collection and visualization of pollution data. Developed together with my friends as a part of our bachelor's thesis at Uppsala Universitet.

[Placeholder]

The Challenge

The core challenge of this project stems from the limitations of traditional urban pollution monitoring systems, which are typically expensive, scarce, and mounted on high rooftops far from the ground-level areas where people actually breathe. To address this, the project set out to design and test a denser, more affordable alternative using a ground-level LoRa node-gateway network in Uppsala, Sweden. This required overcoming two primary hurdles: a technical challenge to prove that low-power LoRa radio devices could maintain stable connectivity and range despite the heavy structural obstructions of a dense urban environment, and a scientific challenge to capture and analyse simultaneous air quality data to demonstrate exactly how much rooftop sensors underestimate the actual pollution affecting people on the street.

Technical Details

  • The MINTS project utilised low-power LoRa radio technology to establish a ground-level node-gateway network capable of medium-to-long-range communication through urban obstructions. The system integrated specialised hardware sensors to simultaneously capture PM1 and PM2.5 particulate matter concentrations. The entire end-to-end ecosystem was developed as an open-source project, with custom node and gateway firmware, custom communications protocol, a dedicated backend database, and a frontend visualization interface.
